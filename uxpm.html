You might also want to know why I like UX and how I got interested in it.

I've always been curious about people. I got a degree in anthropology because learning about humans comes naturally for me.

I had a job working on a helpdesk where I got calls and emails from people who weren't able to understand how to use a third-party database site. Over the five years I was there I spent hundreds of hours answering questions and training them, and knew there had to be a way to make sites that were intuitive and easy to use. I left my job and went to a bootcamp for front-end development, but while I was there I realized it's not a developer's job to care about the user!

Cue my love for UX!

It takes my interest in people and mixes in technology and creativity, so it's a much better fit.




I'm new to UX, but I've been learning a lot, and I have soft skills that help me listen to users.
