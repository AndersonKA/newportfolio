Global Service Design Jam Summary

What is this?

The Global Service Design Jam is a 48 hour project where teams from all over the world research and prototype a product that helps people.

Why did I make this?

I joined the Nashville GSJam team and worked with five teammates and five facilitators to come up with our solution.

What was the result?

We created the paper prototype of an app and a booth that connect people to resources when they need them. Our idea is that a group of supporters (charities, local governments, private citizens) will sign up for the service. Then a person who needs assistance uses the app or a booth to get in contact with the supporters and can request help with anything from housing to an urgent financial situation.

What was my role?

  • I brainstormed ideas for our project
  • I was the notetaker for our guerilla interviews
  • I handwrote everything on our app prototype
  • I helped come up with the name (Support Link)

What did I learn?

I didn't know much about service design before, and this was a great way to get involved. I also learned how much fun it can be to create a project with a group.
